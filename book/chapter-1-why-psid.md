# Chapter 1: Why PSID ?


Why PSID?

The Panel Study of Income Dynamics is the most interesting dataset in the world.

The PSID is one of the longest longitudinal studies in the world. Begun in 1968, the PSID has been interviewing the same people, and then their children, and then their children's children, for more than 50 years. In fact, for some families there are more than four generations of data available. In 1968 they interviewed 4,800 families; by 2023 more than 9,100 families were being followed. Some datasets interview anonymous individuals at a single moment in time. Some datasets follow an individual over a period of time. I am not aware of another dataset that follows thousands of families across generations.

The PSID lets one study the impact of families on the individual. Among so many other things, the PSID lets one examine the educational attainment of families over generations.  The Three Generation Family Explorer demonstrates this with actual families.

A quick check of Google Scholar tells us that between 2022 and 2026 the "Panel Study of Income Dynamics" has been cited about 43,000 times while "census.gov" was cited 644,000 times. I think the reason is that the PSID is not easy to work with. It is a longitudinal dataset meant to be read in SAS. I do not think there is a free version of SAS. And I at least, found the data difficult to work with in Python. R was no easier.

Variable search is a challenge. A search for Education returns 767 results. Home Ownership returns 1,714 results.

I wrote this book hoping to help others get their feet onto the first rung of the PSID ladder using Python with Google Colab.

The PSID has a great website with lots of documentation and carefully made videos, but we are going to skip all of that. For all of the effort that clearly went into them, they were of no help to me. We are going to download the data and then analyze it. Actually, to be truthful, we are going to go about things in reverse. We are going to start by analyzing a finished dataset. And then, if that sort of thing interests you, you can look at the next chapters to see how we arrived at that point.
